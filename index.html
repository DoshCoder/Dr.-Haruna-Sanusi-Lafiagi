Academic Biography

Dr. Haruna Sanusi Lafiagi is a dedicated scholar and educator in the field of Islamic Studies. His academic journey is marked by a deep commitment to the critical study of Islamic tradition. As a faculty member at Al-Hikmah University, he contributes significantly to the moral and academic development of students, emphasizing focus, discipline, and integrity.

He is a native of Lafiagi in the Edu Local Government Area of Kwara State and is recognized for his inspiring personal story of perseverance. He is an alumnus of Al-Hikmah University and furthered his postgraduate studies at the University of Ilorin.

Research Focus & Scholarly Contributions

Dr. Haruna's research provides critical insights into classical Islamic sciences and their contemporary relevance. His work demonstrates rigorous engagement with primary Islamic sources and modern scholarly discourse.

Hadith Sciences

Critical analysis of Hadith documentation, authentication methodologies (Isnād), and scholarly discourse surrounding tradition. This includes examining historical chains of transmission and evaluating the reliability of narrators.

Islamic Jurisprudence (Fiqh)

Principles of Islamic law (Usul al-Fiqh) and their application to contemporary issues. Research focuses on comparative jurisprudence and the methodological foundations of legal reasoning in Islamic tradition.

Islamic Thought

Engaging with historical and modern developments in Islamic theology and philosophy. This includes exploring the intersection of traditional scholarship with contemporary intellectual challenges.

Select Publications

Dr. Haruna actively contributes to academic discourse through peer-reviewed publications. His work is characterized by thorough source analysis and balanced scholarly critique.

Publication Journal/Forum Key Focus Area
A Critical Analysis of Abū Rayyah's Perspectives on Ḥadīth Recording MAQOLAT: Journal of Islamic Studies (2024) Examines 20th-century critiques of Hadith collection, evaluating their methodological approaches and evidence.
Towards a Sustainable Egalitarian Society in Nigeria: A Reflection on the Leadership Style of Umar bn al-Khaṭṭāb Conference Proceeding (2018) Islamic political thought and leadership models applied to contemporary Nigerian society.
An Analytical Discourse on the Language of the Friday Sermon (Jumu'ah Khutbah) Academic Forum (2015) Islamic oratory and effective religious communication in modern contexts.
Methodological Issues in Contemporary Fiqh Research: A Critical Review Journal of Islamic Law & Society (2022) Examination of research methodologies in contemporary Islamic jurisprudence studies.
Hadith Authentication in the Digital Age: Challenges and Opportunities International Journal of Islamic Studies (2021) Exploring the impact of digital tools on traditional Hadith verification methodologies.
